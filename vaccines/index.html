Notes

NHS England publish vaccine data weekly by NHS Improvement integrated care systems (ICS) and sustainability and transformation partnerships (STP). They publish count of vaccinations by age band (not all age bands - it varies week-to-week) by dose and by Integrated Care Systems (ICSs)/Sustainability and Transformation Partnerships (STPs). We've used ONS population estimates for CCGs (mid-2019) to calculate how many people are in the different age categories in each ICS/STP. This allows us to work out the percentage of each age group vaccinated. In the maps, yellow means more and purple means less.

From 27th January all data given by NHS England will include vaccinations administered in Hospital Hubs, Local Vaccination Service sites (e.g. GP practices) and Vaccination Centres. This data is as reported from the National Immunisation Management Service (NIMS), and is classified as management information. NIMS is the System of Record for the NHS COVID-19 vaccination programme in England.

Although some areas appear to have greater than 100% with the first dose of the vaccine this is most likely due to using 2019 estimates of populations in each age group and each area. If the real population is lower than those estimates it can give figures that are greater than 100%.
